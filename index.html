Origin of Ceylon Cinnamon

True Cinnamon – Cinnamomum verum

The only authentic "True Cinnamon" natively grown in Sri Lanka, prized globally for its heritage and chemical purity.

Ceylon cinnamon, scientifically known as Cinnamomum verum J. Presl (also referred to as Cinnamomum zeylanicum), is recognized as "true cinnamon" and is primarily grown in Sri Lanka. The commonly used alternative, cassia (Cinnamomum cassia or Cinnamomum aromaticum), differs significantly in composition, flavor intensity, and safety profile.

Sri Lanka has been at the center of the global cinnamon trade for centuries, with deep historical links to ancient trade routes and long-established cultivation and processing practices. Today, Ceylon cinnamon remains one of the country's signature agricultural products, known for its origin authenticity, consistent quality, and strong international reputation.

Structure & Composition

Safety Profile – Low Coumarin

Contains negligible coumarin levels compared to Cassia, making it the preferred choice for regular health and culinary use.

From a scientific and commercial perspective, Ceylon cinnamon is distinguished by its thin, multi-layered quill structure, light tan-brown color, delicate aroma, and mild, naturally sweet flavor. These characteristics make it especially suitable for premium food, beverage, and wellness applications where balance and refinement are important.

The primary natural compounds in Ceylon cinnamon include cinnamaldehyde, (E)-cinnamyl acetate, and cinnamic acid, which contribute to its characteristic aroma and functional properties.

Health & Safety

Refined Flavor – Mild & Sweet

Distinguished by its multi-layered quills and a delicate, aromatic profile that enhances flavors without being overpowering.

A key difference between Ceylon cinnamon and cassia lies in coumarin content. Cassia cinnamon may contain higher levels of coumarin, whereas Ceylon cinnamon contains only very low levels. This makes it a preferred choice for applications involving regular consumption and controlled ingredient profiles.

Ceylon cinnamon has been widely studied for its natural properties, including antioxidant and antimicrobial activity. While research continues to explore its potential role in areas such as metabolic health, it is primarily valued as a natural food ingredient rather than a medical treatment.

Flavor & Commercial Value

Source Direct – Export Ready

Traditional Sri Lankan processing expertise ensures high essential oil content and adherence to international quality standards.

Due to its mild and refined flavor, Ceylon cinnamon allows manufacturers to achieve balanced formulations with reduced reliance on harsher cinnamon varieties. It is widely used in bakery products, teas, spice blends, desserts, and premium food applications.

For buyers and product developers, Ceylon cinnamon offers clear advantages: improved flavor control, suitability for regular-use formulations, strong origin recognition, and consistent quality supported by traditional Sri Lankan processing expertise. These factors make it a reliable and premium choice for long-term sourcing and product positioning.
